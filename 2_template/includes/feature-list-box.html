Feature explanation

  • .COM Registration Period - this is the maximum number of years your domain can be registered for; you can get your .COM domain for up to 10 years at a time in advance;
  • .COM Transfer - it refers to changing your .COM domain`s registrar; normally 1 year is added to the domain`s expiry date upon successful completion of the transfer; an EPP authorization key (obtainable from the losing registrar) is required for your .COM transfer;
  • .COM Registrar-Lock - it enables the domain owner to lock his/her domain name in order to avoid unauthorized, or accidental changes to the domain name;
  • .COM Edit WHOIS - it allows the publicly displayed WHOIS information of the domain name to be edited at any given moment;
  • .COM ID Protection - this option allows protection of the publicly available WHOIS information of your domain by replacing it with fictitious details.
